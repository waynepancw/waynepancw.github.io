About Me

My doctoral research works on modeling physiological and biomechanical signals (PPG, EMG, IMU) to extract actionable insights in wearable and robotic platforms. I also build autonomous systems independently, including deploying reinforcement learning policies on quadrupeds and creating a vehicle digital twin with a live data pipeline. Passionate about advancing wearable health technologies, assistive robotics, and embodied AI by integrating physics-informed models on real-world hardware.

Throughout my academic journey, I've developed expertise in physics-informed machine learning and nonlinear systems theory for wearable sensor data, enabling data-efficient and interpretable models for robotic state estimation and control, and healthcare applications.
